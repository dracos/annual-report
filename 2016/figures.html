2016 in figures

26 human beings and one bot, crunching hundreds of thousands of lines of code and working with scores of organisations around the world. It’s not all about the numbers, but they do give a good overview.

26 mySociety team members

Two fresh new developers, a sparkling sysadmin, and a splendid research associate joined the team in 2016.

7 new board members

Phew! Thanks to some expansion at the top, we can call on the expertise of 4 new trustees, and 3 new non-executive directors to help plot mySociety’s course.

[?????] code commits

Every time we press the button marked ‘commit’, we’re making changes and additions to our code. This figure’s wildly up on last year’s (15,723) because of the diligent little EveryPolitician Bot. Find out more about that later.

[?????] tickets closed

If you report a problem or suggest a feature, we open a ticket. Then we fix things. ‘Tickets closed’ is one highly satisfying measure of improvement.

58

Applications from prospective partners around the world

10

New partner sites around the world. From Liberia to Costa Rica, and plenty of countries between.

We’re so glad to see the concept of civic technology spreading and doing good all over the world, and we wish we could work with every single one of the organisations who applied for our development help. Sadly, there are only so many hours in the day and only so many developers in mySociety: but until that changes, our vibrant online communities and message groups are there to give you support online.

[??] major pieces of research conducted

Focus groups, Facebook ads, data investigations and on-the-ground visits help us ensure we’re actually making a difference.

This year, we evaluated citizen engagement and investigated civic initiatives in New Zealand and Australia. But we also conducted targeted research in FOI, Democracy, and Better Cities - and that’s also in the sections below.

136 people attended TICTeC 2016

¡Viva la tecnologia cívica! Our annual conference on the Impacts of Civic Technology was held in Barcelona this year. Churros and civic tech are a great mix.

5 UK councils partnered with

Our FixMyStreet for Councils product is better by magnitudes, thanks to the councils who generously gave us their insights and experience.

17 team member towns
